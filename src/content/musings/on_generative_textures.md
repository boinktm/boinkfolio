I keep coming back to noise not as an effect, but as a design language.

When a texture feels too clean, it usually means every surface is obeying a perfect rule. Real materials almost never do that. Grain shifts, roughness drifts, and edge wear appears in places that are not symmetrical.

For this pass, I focused on combining two ideas:

- **Low-frequency breakup** for major material variation.
- **High-frequency detail** for believable surface response under light.

The key is controlling contrast. If every layer is strong, the result becomes visual static. If each layer is subtle and staged, the material starts to feel intentional and physical.

Current rule of thumb: block large forms first, then add micro detail only where the silhouette and focal areas need support.
