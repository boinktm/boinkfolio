I treat sound beds like room design.

A mix that feels "flat" usually has all of its important elements in the same depth plane. To fix that, I build a simple vertical stack:

- **Foreground**: distinct elements that carry intent.
- **Mid layer**: motion and glue.
- **Background**: tonal atmosphere and air.

The biggest improvement came from subtracting, not adding. Leaving frequency and timing gaps makes the scene feel larger because the listener can resolve where things live.

When it works, the listener should feel like they can walk through the sound instead of just hearing it.
